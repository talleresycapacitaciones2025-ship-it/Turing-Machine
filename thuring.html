How it works: The machine starts at the leftmost bit and scans right until it finds the end of the number. Then it moves left, flipping 1s to 0s (carry) until it finds a 0, which it flips to 1. If it hits a blank, it writes a 1 (overflow). This is exactly how you add 1 in binary!
